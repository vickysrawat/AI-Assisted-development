---

## IDENTIFIED AI PATTERNS

Before the rewrites — here are the five specific patterns that made the original narration sound generated rather than spoken:

**Pattern 1 — Perfect rhetorical symmetry**
> *Original:* "Five responsibilities, five walls, in the order we hit them."
> *Problem:* Real speakers don't count things out loud with that kind of precision. It sounds like a bullet-pointed list read aloud.

**Pattern 2 — Bumper-sticker summary phrases**
> *Original:* "Coder to conductor — that's the promotion." / "Trust, as we'd already learned the hard way, does not survive an audit."
> *Problem:* These read beautifully on paper. Spoken out loud they land like a TED talk trying too hard.

**Pattern 3 — Essay-style transition signposting**
> *Original:* "Which sets up the next problem." / "That's the last act. Let me bring it home."
> *Problem:* Written essays use these as structural glue. A speaker discovers the next thing; they don't announce that they're about to announce it.

**Pattern 4 — Abstract passive causation**
> *Original:* "The incentive did." / "The window was simply empty." / "That distinction is the whole game."
> *Problem:* These are elegant on paper but they hide the human. Real speakers say "nobody remembered to check" not "the window was empty."

**Pattern 5 — Over-cadenced lists with identical rhythm**
> *Original:* "Intent — what are we building, and why... Context — where it lives... Examples — you show instead of tell... And Acceptance —"
> *Problem:* The identical grammatical pattern in every item signals a template, not a person thinking out loud.
